# Flower-Scented Tea — Reprocessed Tea (花茶 — 再加工茶)

[中文版](README_cn.md) | [← Back to Home](../../README.md)

## Overview

Jasmine tea and other flower-scented teas are among the most famous teas in China, but they **do not belong to the six major tea categories**. They are classified as **reprocessed tea (再加工茶)** because non-tea ingredients — such as jasmine flowers — are added during production.

## What Makes It "Reprocessed"?

Flower-scented tea starts with a base tea (usually green tea, sometimes white or oolong), which is then scented by layering it with fresh flowers. The tea leaves absorb the fragrance of the flowers through a process called **scenting (窨制)**. This process may be repeated multiple times — the more rounds, the more fragrant the tea.

## Famous Flower-Scented Teas

- **Jasmine Tea (茉莉花茶)** — The most popular flower tea. Green tea base scented with jasmine blossoms. Fujian and Guangxi are major producers.
- **Rose Tea (玫瑰花茶)** — Tea scented or blended with rose petals, offering a sweet floral taste.
- **Osmanthus Tea (桂花茶)** — Scented with osmanthus flowers, producing a delicate, sweet fragrance.

## Brewing Guide

- **Water temperature:** 80–90°C
- **Tea-to-water ratio:** 3g per 150ml
- **Steeping time:** 2–3 minutes
- **Teaware:** Glass cup or porcelain cup (to enjoy the aroma)

Of course, flower-scented tea is also simply delicious!
