# Tea Production Regions in China (中国茶叶产区)

[中文版](README_cn.md) | [← Back to Home](../../README.md)

## Overview

China has numerous tea-producing regions. Almost every province in southern China grows tea. The most famous production regions include **Fujian, Yunnan, Sichuan, Zhejiang, Anhui**, and more.

## Major Tea Regions

| Province | Famous Teas | Tea Categories |
|----------|------------|----------------|
| Fujian (福建) | Tieguanyin, Da Hong Pao, Lapsang Souchong, Fuding White Tea, Jasmine Tea | Oolong, Black, White, Flower |
| Yunnan (云南) | Pu'er (Sheng & Shou), Dianhong | Dark, Black, Green |
| Zhejiang (浙江) | Longjing (Dragon Well), Anji White Tea | Green |
| Anhui (安徽) | Huangshan Maofeng, Keemun, Taiping Houkui, Liu'an Guapian | Green, Black |
| Sichuan (四川) | Mengding Ganlu, Mengding Huangya, Sichuan Border Tea | Green, Yellow, Dark |
| Hunan (湖南) | Junshan Yinzhen, Anhua Dark Tea | Yellow, Dark |
| Jiangsu (江苏) | Biluochun | Green |
| Guangdong (广东) | Fenghuang Dancong | Oolong |
| Guangxi (广西) | Liubao Tea | Dark |
| Henan (河南) | Xinyang Maojian | Green |
| Hubei (湖北) | Enshi Yulu, Laoqingzhuan | Green, Dark |

## Teas Named After Their Region

Many Chinese teas are named after their place of origin:

- **Dianhong (滇红)** — "Dian" is the historical name for Yunnan; this is Yunnan's black tea.
- **Minhong (闽红)** — "Min" is the historical name for Fujian; this is Fujian's black tea.
- **Pu'er Tea (普洱茶)** — Named after Pu'er city in Yunnan.
- **Rock Tea / Yancha (岩茶)** — Oolong tea from the rocky terrain of Wuyi Mountains, Fujian.
- **Liubao Tea (六堡茶)** — Named after Liubao town in Guangxi.

And many more...
