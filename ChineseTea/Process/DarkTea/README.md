# Dark Tea (黑茶)

[中文版](README_cn.md) | [← Back to Home](../../../README.md)

## Overview

Dark tea is a **post-fermented** tea, meaning it undergoes microbial fermentation after the initial processing. Microorganisms participate in the transformation of the tea leaves, causing significant chemical changes that give dark tea its distinctive aged, earthy character. A good dark tea produces a translucent, clear liquor — clarity is a sign of quality.

## Production Process

**Full process:** Picking → Kill-green → Rolling → **Piling/Fermentation (渥堆)** → Drying → (Optional: Compression)

The key step is **piling (渥堆)**, where tea leaves are piled in a warm, humid environment to promote microbial fermentation. This process can take weeks to months and is what distinguishes dark tea from all other categories.

## Famous Dark Teas

| Tea | Region | Characteristics |
|-----|--------|----------------|
| Ripe Pu'er (熟普) | Yunnan | Smooth, earthy, ages well over decades |
| Anhua Dark Tea (安化黑茶) | Hunan | Includes Fuzhuan brick with distinctive "golden flowers" (beneficial fungi) |
| Laoqingzhuan (老青砖) | Hubei | Traditional brick tea, robust and mellow |
| Liubao Tea (六堡茶) | Guangxi | Betel nut aroma, smooth and sweet |
| Sichuan Border Tea (四川边茶) | Sichuan | Historically traded to Tibet, strong and durable |

## Aging and Storage

Dark tea is one of the few teas that **improves with age**. Properly stored dark tea can be kept for decades, with the flavor becoming smoother, deeper, and more complex over time. Storage should be:
- In a dry, well-ventilated area
- Away from strong odors
- At stable room temperature

## Brewing Guide

- **Water temperature:** 95–100°C (boiling water)
- **Tea-to-water ratio:** 5–8g per 100ml
- **Steeping time:** Start with 10–15 seconds, gradually increase
- **Teaware:** Yixing clay teapot or porcelain gaiwan
- **Tip:** Rinse the tea with a quick hot water wash before the first infusion to "awaken" the leaves.
