# Green Tea (绿茶)

[中文版](README_cn.md) | [← Back to Home](../../../README.md)

## Overview

Green tea is the most widely produced and consumed tea in China. It is an **unoxidized** tea — the key step in its production is "kill-green" (杀青), which halts oxidation early to preserve the fresh, vegetal character of the leaves.

## Production Process

The core step that defines green tea is **kill-green (杀青)**, which uses heat to deactivate the enzymes that cause oxidation. Based on the kill-green method, green tea is classified into four types:

| Type | Chinese | Method | Representative Teas |
|------|---------|--------|-------------------|
| Pan-fired | 炒青 | Stir-fried in a hot wok | Longjing (Dragon Well), Biluochun |
| Baked | 烘青 | Dried in an oven | Huangshan Maofeng, Taiping Houkui |
| Steamed | 蒸青 | Steamed to fix color | Enshi Yulu, Japanese-style greens |
| Sun-dried | 晒青 | Dried under sunlight | Yunnan sun-dried green (Sheng Pu'er base) |

**Full process:** Picking → Kill-green → Rolling → Drying

## Famous Green Teas

- **Longjing (龙井)** — From Hangzhou, Zhejiang. Flat, smooth leaves with a chestnut-like aroma.
- **Biluochun (碧螺春)** — From Dongting, Jiangsu. Tightly rolled, with a fresh floral fragrance.
- **Huangshan Maofeng (黄山毛峰)** — From Anhui. Delicate, slightly sweet with an orchid-like aroma.
- **Taiping Houkui (太平猴魁)** — From Anhui. Unusually large, flat leaves with a unique orchid fragrance.
- **Xinyang Maojian (信阳毛尖)** — From Henan. Fine, tightly rolled with a fresh, clean taste.

## Brewing Guide

- **Water temperature:** 75–85°C (do not use boiling water)
- **Tea-to-water ratio:** 3g per 150ml
- **Steeping time:** 1–3 minutes
- **Teaware:** Glass cup or porcelain gaiwan (to appreciate the leaves unfurling)

## Health Benefits

Green tea is rich in catechins and tea polyphenols. It is associated with antioxidant effects, metabolism support, and mental alertness due to its moderate caffeine and L-theanine content.
