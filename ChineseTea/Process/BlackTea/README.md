# Black Tea (红茶)

[中文版](README_cn.md) | [← Back to Home](../../../README.md)

## Overview

Black tea is one of the most popular and widely consumed types of tea around the world. It is a **fully oxidized** tea, known for its bold, robust flavor and dark reddish infusion. While it is called "black tea" in English, in Chinese it is known as **红茶 (hóngchá)** — literally "red tea" — named after the reddish color of its liquor.

## Production Process

**Full process:** Picking → Withering → Rolling → **Full Oxidation** → Drying

The key difference from other teas is the **full oxidation** step, where rolled tea leaves are spread out in a warm, humid environment until they turn a deep copper-red color.

## Classification by Process

Black tea is classified into three categories by processing method:

| Type | Chinese | Description | Representative Teas |
|------|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| Small-leaf (Xiaozhong) | 小种红茶 | Originated in Wuyi Mountains, traditional pine-smoke drying | Lapsang Souchong (正山小种) |
| Gongfu Black Tea | 工夫红茶 | Refined, meticulous processing | Keemun (祁门红茶), Dianhong (滇红) |
| CTC / Broken Black Tea | 红碎茶 | Machine-crushed for strong extraction | Used in tea bags and blends |

## Famous Black Teas

- **Lapsang Souchong (正山小种)** — From Wuyi Mountains, Fujian. The world's first black tea. Traditional versions have a distinctive pine-smoke aroma.
- **Keemun (祁门红茶)** — From Anhui. Known as one of the world's three great black teas, with a wine-like aroma and mellow taste.
- **Dianhong (滇红)** — From Yunnan. Made from large-leaf cultivars, producing a rich, golden-tipped tea with a honey-sweet flavor.
- **Jin Jun Mei (金骏眉)** — A premium Fujian black tea made entirely from buds, with a sweet, honeyed character.

## Brewing Guide

- **Water temperature:** 85–95°C
- **Tea-to-water ratio:** 3–5g per 150ml
- **Steeping time:** 2–3 minutes (western style) or 5–10 seconds (gongfu style)
- **Teaware:** Porcelain gaiwan, glass cup, or teapot
