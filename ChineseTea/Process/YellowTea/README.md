# Yellow Tea (黄茶)

[中文版](README_cn.md) | [← Back to Home](../../../README.md)

## Overview

Yellow tea is one of the rarest and least-known tea categories. It is produced using a process very similar to green tea, with one crucial additional step: **sealed yellowing (闷黄)**. This extra step gives the tea a mellower, smoother taste compared to green tea, with reduced astringency.

## Production Process

**Full process:** Picking → Kill-green → Rolling → **Sealed Yellowing (闷黄)** → Drying

The sealed yellowing step involves wrapping the tea leaves in cloth or paper while still warm and moist, allowing a slow, gentle micro-oxidation. This process:
- Reduces the grassy flavor found in green tea
- Creates a more mellow, sweet taste
- Gives the leaves and liquor a distinctive yellowish hue

## Famous Yellow Teas

- **Junshan Yinzhen (君山银针)** — From Hunan. Made entirely from buds, one of China's most famous teas.
- **Mengding Huangya (蒙顶黄芽)** — From Sichuan. Delicate buds with a sweet, chestnut-like flavor.
- **Huoshan Huangya (霍山黄芽)** — From Anhui. Fresh, mellow, with a clean finish.

## Market Status

Compared to other tea categories, yellow tea has a relatively small market share. Its production is labor-intensive and the sealed yellowing step requires precise control, making it more expensive and less widely available. However, this rarity makes it a prized choice for tea connoisseurs.

## Brewing Guide

- **Water temperature:** 80–85°C
- **Tea-to-water ratio:** 3g per 150ml
- **Steeping time:** 1–2 minutes
- **Teaware:** Glass cup or porcelain gaiwan
