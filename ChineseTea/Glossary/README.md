# Tea Glossary — Common Chinese Tea Terms (茶叶术语表)

[中文版](README_cn.md) | [← Back to Home](../../README.md)

## Processing Terms

| Term | Pinyin | Chinese | Definition |
|------|--------|---------|-----------|
| Kill-green | Shā qīng | 杀青 | Heating tea leaves to halt oxidation; the defining step of green tea |
| Withering | Wěi diāo | 萎凋 | Allowing freshly picked leaves to lose moisture and soften |
| Rolling | Róu niǎn | 揉捻 | Shaping the leaves and breaking cell walls to release juices |
| Sealed Yellowing | Mèn huáng | 闷黄 | Wrapping warm, moist leaves to allow slow micro-oxidation (yellow tea) |
| Shaking/Bruising | Yáo qīng | 摇青 | Gently tossing leaves to bruise edges and promote partial oxidation (oolong) |
| Piling | Wò duī | 渥堆 | Piling tea for microbial post-fermentation (dark tea) |
| Roasting | Bèi huǒ | 焙火 | Applying heat to develop flavor and reduce moisture (oolong, dark tea) |
| Scenting | Yìn zhì | 窨制 | Layering tea with flowers to absorb fragrance (flower tea) |
| Drying | Gān zào | 干燥 | Final moisture removal to stabilize the tea |
| Oxidation | Yǎng huà | 氧化 | Enzymatic browning of tea leaves when exposed to air |
| Fermentation | Fā jiào | 发酵 | In Chinese tea context, often refers to both oxidation and microbial fermentation |

## Teaware Terms

| Term | Pinyin | Chinese | Definition |
|------|--------|---------|-----------|
| Gaiwan | Gài wǎn | 盖碗 | A lidded bowl for brewing tea; the most versatile Chinese teaware |
| Yixing Teapot | Yí xīng hú | 宜兴壶 | Unglazed clay teapot from Yixing, Jiangsu; absorbs tea flavor over time |
| Fair Cup | Gōng dào bēi | 公道杯 | A pitcher used to ensure even tea concentration before serving |
| Tasting Cup | Pǐn míng bēi | 品茗杯 | Small cup for drinking tea |
| Aroma Cup | Wén xiāng bēi | 闻香杯 | Tall, narrow cup used to appreciate a tea's aroma |
| Tea Tray | Chá pán | 茶盘 | A tray that catches overflow water during gongfu brewing |
| Tea Pet | Chá chǒng | 茶宠 | Small clay figurine placed on the tea tray, nourished with tea |

## Tasting & Evaluation Terms

| Term | Pinyin | Chinese | Definition |
|------|--------|---------|-----------|
| Liquor | Chá tāng | 茶汤 | The brewed liquid; ideally translucent and clear |
| Hui Gan | Huí gān | 回甘 | A sweet aftertaste that returns after swallowing |
| Sheng Jin | Shēng jīn | 生津 | The sensation of saliva production triggered by tea |
| Cha Qi | Chá qì | 茶气 | The "energy" or body sensation experienced from tea |
| Yan Yun | Yán yùn | 岩韵 | The unique "rock rhyme" mineral taste of Wuyi rock teas |
| Mountain Flavor | Shān chǎng | 山场 | Terroir — how growing conditions affect tea character |

## Brewing Method Terms

| Term | Pinyin | Chinese | Definition |
|------|--------|---------|-----------|
| Gongfu Brewing | Gōng fu chá | 功夫茶 | A precise brewing method using high leaf-to-water ratio and short steeps |
| Rinse / Wake Tea | Xǐ chá | 洗茶/润茶 | A quick initial pour to "awaken" compressed or aged tea |
| Western Brewing | Dà bēi pào | 大杯泡 | Brewing with lower leaf ratio and longer steep (mug style) |
| Grandpa Style | — | 大缸泡 | Leaves directly in a large cup, refilled with hot water throughout the day |
